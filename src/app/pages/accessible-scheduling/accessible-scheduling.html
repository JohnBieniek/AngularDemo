The operational problem

Scheduling care is more than filling empty time slots.

Coordinators need to reconcile staff availability, travel, qualifications, client requests, overtime, weekend rotation, and assignment compatibility. Doing that by hand is slow, difficult to review, and easy to disrupt when requirements change.

The product response

Make constraints visible, then automate the repetitive work.

Accessible Scheduling models the participants and rules directly, generates candidate shifts and assignments, reports issues, and leaves coordinators with a schedule they can review and adjust.

Constraint-aware planning

Built around the decisions coordinators actually make

The application treats scheduling rules as first-class domain concepts instead of hiding them in spreadsheets and manual checks.

Availability

Match working times and requested coverage before assignments are finalized.

Client compatibility

Represent client needs and employee-to-client compatibility in the schedule.

Travel

Account for the practical movement required between care assignments.

Overtime

Surface weekly workload and over-scheduling concerns during planning.

Weekend rotation

Support alternate-weekend and recurring coverage considerations.

Issue reporting

Return actionable shift and schedule notifications for coordinator review.

Full-stack architecture

Layered for change, deployment, and persistence choices

A browser client talks to Spring controllers backed by domain managers and repository abstractions. Spring profiles support an in-memory development mode or MongoDB, while Cloud Foundry connectors discover bound services in deployed environments.
